Hi, I'm Jieun!

I am a Research Intern at Korea Institute of Science and Technology, working on developing neural network models and learning rules guided by neuroscience. My research focuses on developing neural network models and learning rules guided by neuroscience, with the goal of computationally interpreting these networks to understand their underlying mechanisms.

I received my M.S. in Industrial and Management Engineering from Korea University, South Korea, where I worked with Professor Jungeol Baek on anomaly detection systems and multivariate time-series analysis. I also hold a B.S. in Statistics and Computer Science from Sookmyung Women's University, where I developed strong foundations in statistical modeling and computational methods.

My current research aims to create persistent and robust decision-making systems capable of functioning in complex, unstructured environments. I am particularly interested in how intelligent systems can have profound societal impact in areas such as environmental monitoring, search and rescue operations, and cargo or passenger transportation through neuroscience-inspired approaches.

Research Experience

Computational Model for Spiking Neural Networks and Brain-Inspired Models

May 2024 - Present | Korea Institute of Science and Technology

Developing computational models using spiking neural networks to understand brain functionality and create brain-inspired AI systems. This research focuses on how spatially organized connectivity influences information transfer within motor cortex networks, providing a unique geometric perspective on neural computation. The work on understanding topographic input structures directly relates to developing robust neural representations for advanced AI applications.

Neural ODE Networks for Irregular Time Series Spike Detection

Aug 2023 - Feb 2024 | Georgia Institute of Technology

Developed neural ODE (Ordinary Differential Equation) networks for spike detection in irregular time series data. This approach leverages continuous-time modeling to better capture the temporal dynamics and irregular patterns in complex time-series datasets, improving detection accuracy for spiking events in building sensor data.

Deep Network-based Representation Enhancement for Time Series Data

March 2021 - Feb 2023 | Korea University

Designed deep network-based systems to enhance representation learning for multivariate time-series data. To address the challenge of missing areas in time series data, I proposed transformer-based networks for representation enhancement. This research focused on improving the quality and robustness of learned representations from incomplete temporal data, collaborating with Hyundai on real-world automotive applications.
