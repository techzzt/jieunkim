Jieun Kim

Investigating Trustworthy Machine Learning, Model Reliability & Robustness, Representation Learning, and Brain-Inspired AI

I am a Research Intern at the Korea Institute of Science and Technology (KIST) under the supervision of Dr. Taegon Kim. My computational research focuses on constructing large-scale spiking neuron network (SNN) architectures and understanding circuit dynamics in cortico-basal ganglia loops. By computationally modeling neural synchronization and motor/non-motor symptom comorbidity in Parkinson's disease, I aim to develop robust brain-inspired AI frameworks and adaptive deep brain stimulation (DBS) strategies.

I received my M.S. in Industrial and Management Engineering from Korea University (Advisor: Prof. Jun-Geol Baek), where I engineered multivariate time-series representation architectures and transformer-based missing value imputation models. I hold a B.S. in Statistics & Computer Science from Sookmyung Women's University (Advisor: Prof. Young-Geun Choi).

Korea Institute of Science and Technology (KIST)

Research Intern | Advisor: Dr. Taegon Kim

May 2024 – Feb 2026

Research Focus & Objectives

Investigating topographic cortico-basal ganglia computational circuits to computationally model the emergence of both motor and non-motor symptom comorbidities in Parkinson’s disease (PD). The overarching goal is to understand how altered spiking dynamics and network-level synchronization degrade signal processing in neural systems.

Technical & Modeling Methodology

  • Constructed large-scale Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) incorporating realistic anatomical connectivity, topographic input projections, and synaptic plasticity parameters.
  • Quantitative simulation of population spiking activity and phase-locking neural synchronization across baseline vs. dopamine-depleted pathological states.
  • Extracted dynamic biomarkers to optimize predictive algorithms and metrics for adaptive closed-loop Deep Brain Stimulation (aDBS).
